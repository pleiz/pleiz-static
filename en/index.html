New perspective

“The Web is humanity connected by technology.” — Sir Tim Berners-Lee

This is a website. And it’s made by words, the ones you’re reading or listening to right now. You might be able to do it from another place, another device, another language, in another moment: in your way. But you’re receiving them because they have a space on the Web.

To have a space on the Web means having a voice. To have a voice means visibility, opportunities, presence, existence. We ask ourselves what it means being a digital person. What it means having a voice and a space. What it means not having them.

We want to give an equal space to all people, especially to those who didn’t have the chance yet to express their words. To exist digitally. To have their spaces.

New spaces

  • Secure

  • Accessibles

  • Inclusive

  • Diverse

  • Autonomous

  • Customizable

  • Free

  • By and for everyone

This is our vision as Pleiz: a humane view of our digital spaces. A humane view for spaces that already exist on the Web, for those who are being created, and those who are coming.

It’s time to rethink and build, step by step, the humanity connected by technology: because the Web is for everyone.

Let’s begin.
