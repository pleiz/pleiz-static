New perspective

“The Web is humanity connected by technology.” — Sir Tim Berners-Lee.

This is a website. And it’s made up of words, the ones you’re reading or listening to right now. You might be able to do it from another place, another device, another language, in another moment: in your way. But you’re receiving them because they have a space on the Web.

To have a space on the Web means having a voice. To have a voice means visibility, opportunities, presence, existence. We ask ourselves what it means to be a digital person. What it means to have a voice and a space. What it means to not have them.

We want to give equal space to all people, especially to those who didn’t already have the chance to express their words. To exist digitally. To have their spaces.

New spaces

  • Secure

  • Accessible

  • Inclusive

  • Diverse

  • Autonomous

  • Customizable

  • Free

  • By and for everyone

This is our vision as Pleiz: a humane view of our digital spaces. A humane view for spaces that already exist on the Web, for the ones that are being created, and the ones that are coming.

It’s time to rethink and build, step by step, the humanity connected by technology: because the Web is for everyone.

Let’s begin.
